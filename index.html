Understanding Orthogonal Trajectories

What are Orthogonal Trajectories?

Orthogonal trajectories are families of curves that intersect another family of curves at right angles (orthogonally). In mathematical terms, at every intersection point, the product of their slopes is -1.

Calculation Methodology

  1. Start with given family: F(x, y, C) = 0, where C is the parameter
  2. Differentiate to eliminate C: Obtain dy/dx = f(x, y)
  3. Take negative reciprocal: For orthogonal trajectories, replace dy/dx with -1/f(x, y)
  4. Solve the new differential equation: Integrate to find the orthogonal family

Engineering Applications

Electromagnetic Fields

Electric field lines and equipotential surfaces are orthogonal trajectories

Fluid Dynamics

Streamlines and equipotential lines in potential flow analysis

Heat Transfer

Isotherms and heat flux lines in conduction problems

Topography

Contour lines and gradient directions on topographic maps
